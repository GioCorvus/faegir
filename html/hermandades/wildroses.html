Wild Roses

The Wild Roses are firebrands, rebels, mercenaries, troublemakers, and heroes, depending on who’s telling the story. A band of supernovas tearing through the world, defying the Land of Dawns, the Zenith, and every chain laid upon them by lords, crowns, and blood.

At their center is Zephyr, a young man raised in the gutters of Ebon Crown, carrying old scars the same way he wields kindness like a blade.

But this isn’t a cause. The Wild Roses don’t march under banners or gods. They owe allegiance to no kingdom, and bear no creed but their own. They take in the outcasts, the broken, the untamed, those too stubborn to die and too wild to kneel. There are no open invitations, no trials to earn a place. You don’t join the Roses. You survive long enough, bleed honest enough, burn bright enough, and one day, you find you’re already part of them.

Bound not by law, but by a hunger for freedom and a refusal to be forgotten, they chase their own stories, carve their names into the world, and leave nothing untouched.

If anything unites them, it’s that unyielding, reckless spark, because the world tried to break them, and they made it bloom.
