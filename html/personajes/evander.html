Evander, the Hunter

There was a time his name meant something. Not the kind people sang in taverns or inked into books, but the kind spoken in whispers, behind closed doors. Places where the air felt heavy and the past never stayed buried. His name opened doors, or closed them. He walked among those who shaped the night, and the world bent, just a little, to let him pass.

But that was before.

They took what was dearest to him. Not by chance, not in some careless mistake. It was a choice. A deliberate message written in blood. After that, whatever tied Evander to the life he knew came apart.

He left the capital. Left the Ebon Crown, its ceremonies, its long, endless hunger. He walked toward the far edges of the world, where the light doesn't shine and unspeakable horrors linger. What he is now doesn’t have a name. He moves like someone out of step with the world, hunting the kind of creatures he once called allies, his own kin. Not for pride. Not for peace. Something colder than either.

People say he was important, once, that testing him was a mistake few lived to repeat. But those days are gone. He doesn’t speak of them. And the ones who remember tend to disappear.

Evander isn’t a hero. He’s not a villain, either. He’s the thing that comes when monsters think no one’s left to stop them. A hunter, a devourer. And if the world sleeps easier because of it, it isn’t because of kindness.

It’s because of memory.
Personality

Quiet. Cold when he needs to be. Carries the weight of old losses, though he won’t talk about them. Driven, but it’s hard to tell if it’s justice or revenge. Keeps people at arm’s length. Doesn’t trust easy. There’s a sharp edge under all that quiet, the kind that’s not there by accident. Still, there’s a sense he’s chasing something he’ll never quite catch.
