Gaheris, the Cursed Blade

This one doesn’t make promises. He doesn’t have to. His reputation is enough. A man who hunts curses, slays monsters, and breaks the bonds of magic. But none of it comes without a price. His blonde, dirty hair frames a face that tells the story of a life lived on the edge of something dark. The scars on his skin, the twisting dark veins running down his arms and neck, are a reminder of a pact he never should have made. His eyes, once a bright blue, have dulled. They do not blink at the horrors they have seen, but sometimes, they hold a flicker of regret. Or perhaps, just resolve.

He often wears different kinds of black armour, always worn and battered, its surfaces dull with age and battle. Chains of gold always hang from his chest, each one carrying a relic, glowing faintly with magic, tools for his trade. They are the last remnants of a faith he abandoned long ago, charms meant to ward off corruption.

He is no knight, no hero. He does not fight for glory, nor does he fight to save anyone. Not anymore. He fights because it is all he knows, because monsters are easier to kill than men, because breaking curses is simpler than facing the past. The world has its tales, but Gaheris does not live for them. He is not the one people pray for. He is the one they turn to when there is no one left.

There was once something else. Someone else. Blood in his veins binds him to a history that refuses to die. A name, buried in the ashes of the past. A mother who burned, a shadow cast over everything she left behind. And a brother, one who walks a different road, or perhaps, one whose path was never his to choose.

There is magic inside of him, but it was never his ally. The curse dwells within him, a consequence of desperation, something that claimed more than he ever intended to give. It does not serve him, it does not protect him. But it has kept him alive long enough to finish the job, and that is all he asks of it. He does not believe in redemption. There is only the next hunt, the next curse to break, and when it is done, he will move on to the next. Because that is all he knows how to do.
Personality

Gaheris is reserved, calculating, and fiercely independent. He speaks little, his words blunt and to the point. There’s little patience for weakness, but beneath his cold exterior lies weariness, born from years in the trade. He no longer believes in redemption, only survival, and the next hunt. Yet, despite the curse that drags him down, something still burns within him like a flicker of something long gone, buried beneath the years of wounds and broken faith.
