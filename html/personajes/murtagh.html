Murtagh, the Deathsworn

Murtagh is not the strongest Deathstalker, nor the fastest, nor the most magically gifted. He doesn’t need to be. Where others burn bright and wild, Murtagh is something far more dangerous.

He is precise. Methodical. The perfect soldier.

His blade never trembles. His aim never falters. He is a weapon honed to the edge of impossibility, an executioner whose hands have ended more lives than he can count. Literally: way too many. But while he does not remember his past life, he remembers every single face he has ever killed. Each expression frozen in time, each final moment burned into his mind. They do not haunt him, there is no grief, no guilt, no sorrow. And yet, they remain. A presence in the silence. A tally that never fades.

He does not hesitate. He does not fear. He does not fail.

Theron, the father of all Deathstalkers, calls him his greatest experiment. Not because he is the most powerful, but because he is flawless. No hesitation, no distractions, no desire beyond the mission. The others have weaknesses. Murtagh does not. He does not chatter with his kin, does not seek camaraderie, does not feel the ache of solitude. He exists to serve, and he does so perfectly.

Or at least, he did.

Lately, something is wrong. Not a flaw, no. He is flawless. Not a weakness either: he does not have those. But still, there is something. A hesitation where there should be none. A flicker of recognition in the faces of the dying. A feeling unwanted, in the quiet moments between orders. Like a memory pressing against a door that should have remained locked. Like something inside him is waking up.

And if that happens… if the perfect blade begins to crack… what will be next? What will be left?
